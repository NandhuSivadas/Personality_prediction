About the Big Five

The "Big Five" (or OCEAN model) is the most widely accepted and used model of personality in academic psychology. It describes five broad dimensions of human personality.

O is for Openness

This trait features characteristics such as imagination, insight, and a broad range of interests.

  • High: Very creative, curious, open to new things.
  • Low: Practical, conventional, prefers routine.

C is for Conscientiousness

This dimension includes high levels of thoughtfulness, good impulse control, and goal-directed behaviors.

  • High: Organized, dependable, detail-oriented.
  • Low: Spontaneous, flexible, can be disorganized.

E is for Extraversion

This trait includes characteristics such as excitability, sociability, talkativeness, and assertiveness.

  • High: Outgoing, energetic, gains energy from social situations.
  • Low: Solitary, reserved, gains energy from being alone.

A is for Agreeableness

This dimension includes attributes such as trust, altruism, kindness, affection, and other prosocial behaviors.

  • High: Cooperative, trusting, empathetic.
  • Low: Competitive, skeptical, can be blunt.

N is for Neuroticism

This trait is characterized by sadness, moodiness, and emotional instability. (Also called its opposite: Emotional Stability).

  • High: Experiences stress and anxiety easily, moody.
  • Low: Emotionally stable, resilient, calm.
